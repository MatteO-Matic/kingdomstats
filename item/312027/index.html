Wu Tiger Warriors

Tiger Warriors were the Eastern Wu's palace guards and the princes' personal guards. Robust and well equipped, they were strictly selected from the finest and most loyal of warriors. As they performed defense tasks over long periods, they rarely fought directly. In 215 AD (the twentieth year of the Jian'an era), in the Battle of Hefei, Zhang Liao surprise attacked Xiaoyaojin. At that time, over a thousand Tiger Warriors of Sun Quan resisted the Wei offence under the command of Lu Meng, Gan Ning, Chen Wu, and Ling Tong. The three hundred guards led by Ling Tong all died, but Sun Quan escaped in the end.
