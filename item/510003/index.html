Principes



In the Republic of Rome, this legion referred to citizens of around 30 years old. They had combat experience and their weapons and combat methods were the same as the young soldiers, but because they had more money, they could purchase their own, more complete equipment. Mature soldiers were the core of the legion. In the early three-column Roman Legion battle formation, they formed the second row.
