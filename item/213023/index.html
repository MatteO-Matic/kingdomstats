Chu Rangers



The Jingzhou region was stable and prosperous under the governance of Liu Biao, and so there were many Jingzhou soldiers who were well equipped.
The Chu riders were a private army in southern Jingzhou attached to government troops, and would take on support tasks.
The Chu Rangers were composed of wealthy riding nomads who carried their own weapons and wore leather armor from the government army. Their forces were complex but strong.
They could ride horses at fast speeds and threw flying hammers to attack enemies.
They often attacked the enemy cavalry to lure them out of hiding.
Once the enemy cavalry was isolated, they turned around and attacked.
