Gan Ning

Military general, good at leading close combat infantry and ranged infantry



Gan Ning, also known as Xingba, was from Linjiang, and was officer to the Xiling Prefecture and a noble general. Known as a raider in his youth, he gathered with others to loot ships and was known as the "Brocade Sail Thief". He later quit to learn from philosophers and decided not to join Huang Zu's army, allying with Sun Quan. He performed well in the attack against Huang Zu's army and attacked Cao's army in Wulun during the Battle of the Red Cliffs. Later, in the battle against the south, Gan Ning led 100 people to capture Yiling. He pushed back Guan Yu's army in Yiyang and in the eighteenth year of the Jian'an era, made triumphant gains while leading 100 cavalrymen to attack the Cao camp at night. He achieved repeated military exploits later on, during the Battle of Hefei in Wancheng, but died of illness in the twenty fifth year of the Jian'an era.
