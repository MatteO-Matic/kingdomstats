Shu Repeating Crossbowmen

The repeating crossbow was a kind of mechanical crossbow with light firing power. It came with an arrow compartment that allowed ten arrows to be loaded at once. Shooting could be repeated through the use of a trigger, greatly improving the firing rate. This crossbow had a very short range and limited power, and was generally used by enemy infantry and lightly equipped forces on the battlefield. In order to approach the enemy, crossbow soldiers were equipped with heavy leather armor and pushed forward with large shields. Because these crossbows were heavy and many arrows had to be brought along, they moved slowly and it was difficult for them to quickly escape the front lines. As they inevitably needed to deal with frequent melees, this unit was not widely-used and was soon replaced by large Zhuge crossbows.
