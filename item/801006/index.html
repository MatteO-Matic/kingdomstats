Gongsun Zan

Military general, good at leading close combat cavalry



Gongsun Zan, also known as Bogui, was a warlord near the end of the Eastern Han from Lingzhi, Liaoxi, and was Zhangshi in the early years of Youzhou. He often fought fierce battles with the northern Hu people. In the second year of the Chuping era, Gongsun Zan's 20,000 troops annihilated over 10,000 Qingzhou Yellow Scarves in Dongguang. In the second year of the Chuping era, he began to fight with Yuan Shao for control over Jizhou. The following year, he attacked and killed Liu Yu, was promoted to general, and controlled all of Youzhou. In Yuyang, in the second year of Xingping, Bao Qiu was defeated by Liu Yu's remaining troops and retreated to the capital. Later, he had a confrontation with Yuan Shao and Liu He's troops, and in the fourth year of Jian'an, Gongsun Zan was lured out and defeated by Yuan Shao. He ultimately ended his life through self-immolation.
