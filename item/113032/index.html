Scout Riders



At the beginning of the establishment of the Han Empire, the Xiongnu had threatened the borders for a long time. In order to set up cavalry forces to take them on, the Martial Emperor encouraged people to raise horses. This tradition continued to the end of the Eastern Han. The Han army Scouts were skilled equestrian herdsmen. They had good stamina and vision and could perform tasks in a variety of weather conditions. Scouts usually carried several days of food and water and knew how to observe the enemy closely without being noticed. They wore light clothing and carried only a short dagger-axe and a short sword. If forced into a fight, they could resist to a certain degree and then find a chance to get away. At the end of the Eastern Han dynasty, Scouts were replaced by local warlord rangers.
