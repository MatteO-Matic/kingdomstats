Wuhuan Riders

Wuhuan, also known as Wuwan, was the name for the people who lived on Wuhuan Mountain. They were a group of nomadic herders in the northeast who were good at riding and shooting, and quickly became good at fighting. Starting from the time the Martial Emperor was defending the frontier during the Han Dynasty, they fought in the Central Plains for power on many occasions. In the twelfth year of the Jian'an era, Cao Cao went on an expedition to Wuhuan and destroyed them in Liucheng. He beheaded Wuhuan leader Ta Dun and many people below him. The total amount of those who surrendered reached over 200,000. Later Youzhou, Bingzhou, and over 10,000 Wuhuan troops moved to the Central Plains. The best ones were selected by Cao Cao and became the backbone force of the Cao Wei cavalry. They made their mark on history, as people would long after say "the cavalry from the three Wuwan countries were the best in the world".
