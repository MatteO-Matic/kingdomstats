Wei Crossbow Riders

At the end of the Eastern Han Dynasty, the Central Plains' cavalry changed their roles in the continually escalating Warlord battles. Originally an expedition in Outer Mongolia, the light cavalry who were responsible for resisting captives now took part in the civil war and investigation of damage. These special forces appeared early in the Warring States period. At that time in the Central Plains, many different types of ranged cavalry were developed to resist herdsmen. The pride of the Central Plains, crossbows equipped on cavalrymen was an important innovation. Han Dynasty crossbow cavalry used their hands to draw open light crossbows. Equipped with simple armor, they used sweeping formations to shoot during battle, then they used reloading tactics to persistently harass the enemy. They had good accuracy and their penetration ability was particularly effective in dealing with heavy infantry.
