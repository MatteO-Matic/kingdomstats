Xu Sheng

Military general, good at leading close combat infantry

Also known as Wenxiang, this famous general of the Eastern Wu was known as one of the "Jiang Biao Brave Ministers" along with Cheng Pu, and others. In the twenty-second year of the Jian'an era (217 AD) Cao Cao sent troops to Ruxukou where the Wu army encountered strong winds. The ancient ships were blown towards the enemy, igniting their fear. Only Xu Sheng was able to break through the gale and saw success. In the fifth year of the Huangchu era, (222 AD), he confronted Wei general Cao Xiu with a small amount of soldiers from across the river. As a result of his success, he became known as the Andong general, a noble of Wuhu. In the fifth year of the Huangchu era (224 AD), Cao led troops to the east, so Xu Sheng used diversionary troops to make the enemy retreat, a tactic admired by all.
