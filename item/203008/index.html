Huang Zhong

Balanced leader, can lead close combat infantry and ranged cavalry

Also known as Hansheng, Huang Zhong was a famous general of the Eastern Han from Nanyang. He was originally one of Liu Biao's royal security members, stationed in Changsha. In the thirteenth year of Jian'an, following the fall of the Jingzhou army to Cao Cao, he surrendered once again to Liu Bei. From the eighteenth to the nineteenth year of the Jian'an era, Huang Zhong performed outstandingly during Liu Bei's attack on Yizhou's Liu Zhang and was appointed as the general of enemy invasions. In the twenty-fourth year of the Jian'an era, Huang Zhong led his troops to attack Cao Cao's subordinate, the famous general Xiahou Yuan, during the battle of Mount Dingjun, killing him in the process. After Liu Bei became the emperor of Hanzhong, his title was changed to the later general, a high ranking position. The following year, Huang Zhong died of illness and was known as Ganghou.
