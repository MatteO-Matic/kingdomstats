Shu Repeating Crossbow Cavalry

The repeating crossbow was a kind of mechanical crossbow with light firing power. It came with an arrow compartment that allowed ten arrows to be loaded at once.
Shooting could be repeated through the use of a trigger, greatly improving the rate of fire.
The range of this crossbow was short and its power was limited, so it was generally used by infantry and lightly equipped forces on the battlefield. Shu Repeating Crossbow Cavalry were repeating crossbowmen on horses.
The biggest difference was that they had fast horses. Because the armor of crossbowmen is thick, horses strengthened their mobility.
However, accuracy and reloading speed was decreased, leading to their defeat in later battles by Zhuge's repeating crossbowmen.
